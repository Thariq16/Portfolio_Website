## 1. WHAT THE BEST PM PORTFOLIOS HAVE IN COMMON

### 1.1 Curation over completeness
The consistent advice: portfolios are **greatest hits, not exhaustive lists**. Top examples show 3–5 deeply-told case studies, not 8 shallow cards.
**Your gap:** 8 case studies + a second overlapping "Impact" section = 12 card impressions on one homepage. Quantity is diluting quality. Your four strongest stories by senior-role relevance: (1) Fortude Charlie AI trust pivot, (2) Sling EV ecosystem 0→1, (3) Fortude pricing/GTM, (4) FieldR foundership. Chonk and Motion Miracles can live on the index, not the homepage.

### 1.2 A consistent narrative arc per case study
The benchmark structure across top-rated portfolios: **hook → problem → research → solution → quantified impact**, plus constraints and learnings for senior candidates.
**Your position:** ✅ Your 8-section framework already exceeds the benchmark structurally. This is your strongest asset. The gap is *evidence density*, not structure.

### 1.3 Visual artifacts as proof of process
Strong PM case studies show the work: journey maps, funnel charts, before/after UI, pricing matrices, data visualizations, user flow diagrams. Reviewers explicitly call out visual storytelling as what separates memorable portfolios.
**Your gap:** The Fortude case study is 100% text. You have real artifacts available — Figma prototypes (Sling, FieldR), the IoT command centre, swap-cabinet design photos, the Maldives government deck, FieldR dashboards, even Chonk Instagram creative. None are on the site. **This is the single biggest content gap vs benchmark.**

### 1.4 Clear statement of identity and ask
Best examples open with who you are, your specialization, and what you're looking for (e.g., "B2B SaaS PM with fintech expertise"). ATS and human reviewers qualify candidates in seconds using explicit competency and domain listings.
**Your gap:** The hero says what you've done but never says what you want. No "open to Senior PM / Head of Product roles in KSA & GCC" anywhere. Domains are listed (good) but competencies are scattered.

### 1.5 Don't over-design — but don't under-evidence
Reviewers consistently warn PMs against design-portfolio flashiness; clean and scannable wins. White space, short statements up front, click-through depth.
**Your position:** ✅ Your site's restraint is appropriate. The carousel-fake logo strip is the one element that tries too hard.

### 1.6 Personal dimension as differentiator
Top examples include personal projects/podcasts/side ventures to show range — done as a clearly-labeled secondary layer.
**Your position:** ✅ Football analytics + Rotaract is genuinely differentiated ("Two Dimensions You Won't Find on Most PM CVs" is better than most benchmark examples). Keep. Barca Innovation Hub diploma is an underused credibility asset.

### 1.7 PRD / process documents as competence proof
One benchmark theme for senior credibility: showing you can produce the actual artifacts of the job (PRDs, roadmaps, prioritization frameworks) in lean and full forms.
**Your gap:** Nothing on the site shows a document you've produced. A sanitized PRD excerpt, the complexity-assessment framework from Fortest, or the Sling KPI framework would be high-signal additions for Head-of-Product screening.

## 2. WHAT SEPARATES "SENIOR PM" SITES FROM "PM" SITES

From the FAANG+/senior-level analysis, senior portfolios differ in four ways:

| Dimension | PM-level site | Senior/HoP-level site | You today |
|---|---|---|---|
| Story unit | Features shipped | Business problems owned end-to-end | ✅ Mostly business-level |
| Metrics | Output (launches, users) | Revenue, retention, pipeline, org outcomes | ✅ Strong |
| People | "Worked with engineering" | Team size, reporting line, org scaling | ❌ Missing from homepage |
| Judgment | What was built | Trade-offs, constraints, what they'd change | ✅ In case studies, ❌ not surfaced |
| Third-party proof | None | Testimonials, named enterprise logos, press | ❌ Missing entirely |

**Net:** You're already writing at the senior level in case studies. The site fails to *surface* leadership scale and *prove* it through third parties.

## 3. GCC/KSA-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

1. **Recruiters keyword-search:** "Transferable Iqama," "available immediately," "Riyadh" are search filters — they're on the site ✅ but should also lead the LinkedIn headline.
2. **Arabic:** The case-study pages show an "AR" toggle — if Arabic localization is partial/broken, hide it until complete. A broken Arabic toggle in KSA is worse than none. If completed properly, it's a strong differentiator.
3. **Vision 2030 alignment:** Your EV/battery-swap story maps directly to KSA giga-project and sustainability narratives (you've already built the pitch deck). One line connecting the Sling case study to KSA market replication would resonate with local hiring managers — the site currently doesn't mention KSA market context at all beyond location.
4. **WhatsApp sharing is the GCC referral channel** (your Diriyah and KEC contacts prove it). OG/link-preview correctness ($180K meta bug) matters more here than in most markets.
